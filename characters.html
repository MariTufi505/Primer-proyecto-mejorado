Characters


Dante Alighieri

Dante Alighieri is Both the author and protagonist of the poem. In the story, Dante represents every human soul on a journey toward God. He begins lost in a dark forest, symbolizing moral confusion and sin, and travels through Hell to understand the consequences of evil. His journey is deeply personal yet universal, showing that salvation is possible for anyone willing to repent and seek truth.

Virgil

Dante AlighieriThe ancient Roman poet (author of the Aeneid) chosen as Dante’s guide through Hell and Purgatory. Virgil symbolizes human reason, classical wisdom, and moral discipline. Though wise and virtuous, he cannot enter Paradise because he lived before Christ’s redemption, showing that reason alone, without divine grace, cannot reach ultimate salvation.

Beatrice

Beatrice is Dante’s idealized love, based on the real Beatrice Portinari, who died young. In the poem, she represents divine love, grace, and spiritual beauty. Beatrice sends Virgil to guide Dante and later becomes his guide through Heaven. She explains theological mysteries and leads Dante toward God’s presence, embodying the union of love and divine truth
